## LOGO DIRECTION — LET OPUS DECIDE

We've been working through logo concepts and here's our thinking so far — but we want Opus to use its skills and make the final call. Don't feel locked into any of this.

**What we explored:**
- Pure wordmark — no icons, no shields, no arrows, no military imagery whatsoever
- Two line stacked layout — LEFT FLANK large and bold on top, STRATEGIES smaller beneath
- Weight contrast between the two lines works well
- Navy has been the default color — but it feels too predictable and expected for this name
- We discussed: all black, unexpected accent colors (burnt orange, deep crimson, electric), going against the political color playbook entirely

**What Bencium and Impeccable would say:**
- Navy is banned — too safe, too expected
- One bold unexpected color choice executed perfectly beats four safe choices
- Consider: all black wordmark, or black with a single sharp unexpected accent
- Consider: dramatic size contrast between LEFT FLANK and STRATEGIES
- Consider: all lowercase for unexpected sophistication
- The logo needs to work reversed on dark backgrounds for the website

**Our direction:** Use your skills. Make a decision that's genuinely surprising and ownable. The name is strong enough — the design should have a real point of view.

**Hard rules regardless:**
- NO shields, arrows, military iconography of any kind
- NO navy + white — too predictable
- NO gradients
- NO drop shadows
- Must work on both light and dark backgrounds
- Must scale to favicon size

## VISUAL DIRECTION FOR THE SITE

**Overall feel:** Clean, cinematic, editorial, minimal. Serious without being boring. Confident without being loud. Think high-end production company meets serious political firm.

**What to avoid — strictly:**
- Generic AI aesthetics
- Inter, Roboto, Arial, Space Grotesk — banned
- Purple gradients on white backgrounds — banned
- Navy + white + red patriotic color scheme — banned
- Glassmorphism — banned
- Cookie cutter layouts
- Looking like Foundation Blue, Middle Seat, or any other standard Democratic firm site
- Looking like every other political website — they all look the same

**Typography:**
- Choose something unexpected and characterful
- Pair a distinctive display font with a refined body font
- The headline typography on the hero is the most important typographic decision on the page

**Color:**
- Commit to a cohesive palette with a clear point of view
- Dark backgrounds preferred — cinematic feel
- One dominant color, one sharp accent
- Not the obvious political choices

**Motion and Animation:**
- This is critical — get the animations right
- Full screen video or cinematic hero with purposeful motion
- Staggered page load reveals — orchestrated, not scattered
- Scroll triggered animations
- Hover states that feel considered
- The animation should feel like it belongs to a serious production company — not a startup

**Layout:**
- Unexpected. Not a standard hero/about/services/contact stack
- Generous negative space
- Let the work breathe
- Asymmetry where appropriate
- Grid-breaking elements
